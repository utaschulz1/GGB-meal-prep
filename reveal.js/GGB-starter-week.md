## The GGB Philosophy
- GGB stands for **Grains, Greens, Beans** and it's a way to compose wholesome healthy vegan meals. 
- GGB isn't about restriction; it's about vegan variety: 
    - balanced
    - rich in fibres and nutrients
    - delicious and 
    - often simple and inexpensive. 


### Grain. Green. Bean
- 🌾 **Grain:** The "carb" part of the meal: bread, pasta, rice, corn, sweet potatoes, potatoes, wraps, tacos, nachos, oats, barley, quinoa and so on
- 🥦 **Green:** Any vegetables, even if not green
- 🫘 **Bean:** The "protein" part of the meal: all kind of beans, chickpeas, peas, lentils and tofu

## Global Instructions
- **Start of the week:** The week start at Monday.
- **Locale:** This plan and the recipes is for the European market, grounded in the metric system and products that are available in Europe.
- **Precision:** The given weight in gram has a tolerance of at least 20%. Example: If the recipe says 100 gram of onion, you can use between 80 and 120 gram, the recipe will still work. Be creative, try what works for you.
- **Time estimate:** 1 hour of prep time is plenty for the dishes of this plan and adequate for beginners.
- **Clean food:** Wash all food and peal if necessary before using it. Thoroughly rinse canned bean, since the fluid contains lots of salt and conservation agents.


- **Keep it simple:** You can use canned beans and vegetable broth from powder.
- **Unprocessed** If you want to use food as unprocessed as possible, see the schedule to soak the beans the night before and cook them in the slow cooker in time. Prepare your own vegetable broth. See the additional instructions.
- **Bean weight:** All recipes give the weigth for already cooked beans. Weight conversion: dry beans x 2,2 = cooked beans. Example: 200 gram dry beans make at least 400 gram. Some beans rather have factor 2,5, like Kindney beans.

## Additional Instructions


### How to Cook Beans
* **Pro tip 1:** Add a pinch of soda (bicarb) to the water when cooking beans, it prevents them from not getting soft.
* **Pro tip 2:** Don't add salt. With salt, it takes longer until the beans are cooked.
* **Pro tip 3:** Soak beans at least for 12 hours, black beans and large beans for 24 hours. 


#### Watch your source
Beans have different qualities and cooking times. Sometimes they can be stubborn and stay hard forever. Soda helps, but also the quality of the beans is important. If your beans stay hard, consider buying them elsewhere next time.


#### Use a slow cooker
Mealprep doesn't make sense if you have to watch your beans in the pan for an hour. Use a slow cooker.
1. Thoroughly rinse the beans.
2. Put the beans into the slow cooker, start the slow cooker and set the timer. 
    - Kidney beans, Pinto beans, chick peas 2 hours
    - Black beans, large beans 3 hours
3. Add a pinch of soda.
4. Fill up with boiling water until at least 2cm above
5. Optional: Add 1 tomato, 1 onion, 1 carrot and leftover leek to the beans. You can use the cooking water as broth for recipes like Green Dhal or for soups.
6. When you are ready to use the bean for cooking, put a strainer on a pan to keep the broth. Otherwise strain off the water.


### How to Make Vegetable Broth
* **Option 1:** You can make vegetable broth by using the cooking water of the beans. When cooking beans yourself, add 1 tomato, 1 onion, 1 carrot and leftover leek to the beans and use the cooking water as broth.
* **Option 2:** Cook 1,5 liter of water with 1 tomato, 1 onion, 1 carrot and leftover leek for 30 minutes on low heat. Don't add salt! Strained the broth to keep the liquid and discard the vegetables. 


### How to Make a Salad as a Side Dish
In the recipes of this plan you will find **salad** as an ingredient, as in the shopping list. Salads are nicly fresh and contain extra vitamins. A salad is a great side dish and complements the GGB diet by adding more **Green**. Follow your taste, but keep it simple. Also, consider what kind of ingredients for a salad stay fresh for a couple of days.


In the shopping list, there is lettuce and carrot planed for the side salad. Feel free to change it:
1. Choose your ingredients: lettuce, carrot, tomato, cucumber, onion, bell pepper, anything you like and put it on the shopping list.
2. Wash the incredients and chop or grate them how you like.
3. Add salt, pepper, olive oil and lemon juice or vinegar as you like.
4. Make notes on what you like and how much you need to adjust your shopping list next time.
