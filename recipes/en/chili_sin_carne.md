## The GGB Strategy
This chili hits the **Beans** and **Greens** (if you serve it over spinach) perfectly. It’s designed to be even better the next day, making it the king of meal prep. With nachos, this recipe is enough for 4 big servings, with sweet potatoes or rice it will do for 6 servings.

## Instructions
1. **The Flavor Base:** Heat olive oil in a large pot. Add salt, ground coriander, chili, bay leaves, black cumin, and paprika. Toast for 1 minute until fragrant.
2. **Sauté:** Add onions, garlic, and leeks. Cover and sweat over medium heat until translucent.
3. **The Texture:** Stir in carrots and ginger. Sauté until the mixture starts to lightly caramelize at the bottom of the pot.
4. **Deglaze:** Pour in the red wine (or juice) to scrape up all the flavor from the bottom.
5. **Simmer:** Add the beans and stir well. Cover and let it simmer for 10 minutes to let the flavors meld.
6. **The Bulk:** Add the tomato pulp, corn, and fresh tomatoes. Bring to a boil.
7. **Final Touch:** Stir in the diced bell pepper and lemon zest. Taste and adjust seasoning with more lemon juice or chili if needed.
8. **Finish:** Turn off the heat. Fold in the fresh coriander. Let it sit for 10 minutes before serving. Serve with nachos.
