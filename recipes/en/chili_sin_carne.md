## Why this dish
This chili hits the **Beans** and **Greens** (if you make the salad) perfectly. It’s designed to be even better the next day, making it the king of meal prep. With nachos, this recipe is enough for 4 big servings, with sweet potatoes or rice it will do for 6 servings.

## Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a large pot. Add salt, ground coriander, chili, bay leaves, black cumin, and paprika. Toast for 1 minute until fragrant.
2. Add onions, garlic, and leeks. Cover and sweat over medium heat until translucent.
3. Stir in carrots and ginger. Sauté until the mixture starts to lightly caramelize at the bottom of the pot.
4. Pour in the red wine (or juice) to scrape up all the flavor from the bottom.
5. Add the beans and stir well. Cover and let it simmer for 10 minutes to let the flavors meld.
6. Add the tomato pulp, corn, and fresh tomatoes. Bring to a boil.
7. Stir in the diced bell pepper and lemon zest. Taste and adjust seasoning with more lemon juice or chili if needed.
8. Turn off the heat. Fold in the fresh coriander. Let it sit for 10 minutes before serving. Serve with nachos.
9. Prepare the side salat of your choice, like lettuce and grated carrots with salt, pepper and the rest of the lemon juice.
